### The Five Variables

FLOW (F)
The direction of real capital movement through
the system. Not price. Movement. Where is value
actually going — toward hard assets, toward
sovereign debt, toward digital rails, out of
institutional structures entirely.

CAPACITY — C_eff
The system's actual ability to absorb stress.
Formula: C_eff = T^p x C_elastic + C_viscous + C_rigid

C_elastic: Fed balance sheet intervention capacity.
Collapses non-linearly when Trust falls below 0.50.
C_viscous: Bank credit and normal market function.
Slow to respond. Still functions when elastic is gone.
C_rigid: Hard regulatory floors. Last buffer.
Never flexes. Fails only at system collapse.

When T drops below 0.50 the elastic term
approaches zero regardless of C_raw.
The system loses its primary shock absorber.

PRESSURE (P)
Forces pushing the system toward unstable states.
Energy shocks. Inflation entrenchment.
Monetary policy constraint. Fiscal deterioration.
Geopolitical rupture. Multiple channels firing
simultaneously is the critical configuration.

DRIFT (D)
The structural direction beneath daily noise.
Rate = what is happening today.
State = what the system is becoming.
State > Rate. Always.
Drift is the autocorrelation of State over time.
When State holds the same configuration for
multiple consecutive readings Drift is confirmed.

TRUST (T)
The master variable. Governs all others.
Lambda decay: Trust erodes faster than it heals.
Rho (repair rate) < Lambda (decay rate).
When T falls below 0.50 — elastic capacity gone.
When T falls below 0.30 — capacity fracture.
