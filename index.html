Methodology
Flowstate monitors five structural variables in the global financial system, updated four times daily. These variables combine into a single daily verdict — Stable, Stressed, Critical, or Cascade.
The Five Variables
FLOW
Where is capital actually moving? Not price — direction. Toward hard assets, toward sovereign debt, out of institutional structures, into digital rails. Flow tells you where money is going before the price reflects it.
CAPACITY
How much stress can the system actually absorb? Not the headline number — the effective capacity after accounting for how much of the buffer has already been consumed. A system can look healthy on the surface while its real shock absorption is nearly exhausted.
PRESSURE
What forces are pushing the system toward instability? Energy shocks. Inflation entrenchment. Monetary policy constraints. Fiscal deterioration. When multiple pressure channels activate simultaneously the risk of cascade increases non-linearly.
DRIFT
What direction is the system actually moving? Not what happened today — what is the system becoming. Daily price moves are noise. Drift is the structural direction that only becomes visible when you look at the pattern across multiple readings over time.
TRUST
The master variable. When institutional trust in the monetary system, sovereign debt, or policy credibility degrades — effective capacity collapses. Trust erodes faster than it heals. Once it falls below a critical threshold the system loses its primary shock absorber.
The Six Conditions
A. Energy Shock
Brent crude more than 1.5 standard deviations above its two-year mean. Energy shocks transmit through producer prices to consumer prices within 60–90 days. They also constrain monetary policy — the Fed cannot cut rates to support growth while energy-driven inflation is entrenched.
B. Fear Elevated
The VIX more than 1.5 standard deviations above its two-year mean. Equity markets are pricing elevated uncertainty. Historically the VIX activates after the bond market has already begun repricing — meaning when this condition triggers the stress is already structurally present.
C. Inflation Ceiling
Core CPI at the top of its two-year range. No room left above. The Fed is trapped — it cannot deploy its primary tool without feeding the problem it is trying to solve.
D. Buffer Thin
The Federal Reserve balance sheet below its two-year mean. Two years of quantitative tightening have depleted the elastic capacity that absorbed previous shocks. When this condition is active the system has limited ability to expand its balance sheet without consequences.
E. Bonds Selling
Long-duration Treasury bonds under sustained selling pressure. Investors are demanding higher yields to compensate for inflation risk. When bonds sell alongside equities the safe haven architecture has failed.
F. Banks Stressed
The St. Louis Fed Financial Stress Index above its baseline. The formal banking system is showing stress in its internal plumbing. This is the last active firewall. When this condition activates all six conditions are met and the system is in CASCADE.
The Four States
STABLE 0 to 1 conditions active. All structural variables within normal parameters. The system has meaningful capacity to absorb shocks.
STRESSED 2 to 3 conditions active. Multiple pressure channels elevated simultaneously. The system is absorbing stress but buffer consumption is noted.
CRITICAL 4 to 5 conditions active. Pre-cascade configuration present. Effective capacity significantly degraded. The system is one to two conditions from maximum stress.
CASCADE 6 conditions active. All structural conditions simultaneously active. Maximum stress configuration. The elastic buffer is exhausted.
The Engine Reads In Both Directions
The same conditions that detect structural stress also detect structural recovery.

When conditions deactivate one by one — when the score moves from Critical back toward Stable over consecutive readings — that trajectory is the recovery signal.

Key recovery indicators:
• Energy shock deactivating as supply normalizes.
• Fed balance sheet expanding as elastic capacity rebuilds.
• VIX returning below threshold as fear subsides.
• Copper z-score rising as growth expectations improve.
• Bond market stabilizing as inflation expectations anchor.

The Drift variable captures directional change over time. A consistent downward trend in score across multiple scans signals genuine structural improvement — not a one-day bounce but a real change in the system configuration.

Flowstate does not only measure when things are breaking. It measures when they are healing.
